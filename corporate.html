    Anton - We chose this bold and clear font, because our target audience (dutch football players) are mostly very masculine and chose for easy and clear messages. And also this font will ensure us that the reader can read our titles easily since it is a clear font.  
    Oswald - We chose this font for the subtitles, becuase this is also a clear and bold font but less bold then our heading font. The font is simple but yet it stands out.

  • User interface patterns
    On every page, we use a navigation tab at the top and, on a smaller screen, on the right side. We chose this to make navigating through our website easy. Because our 'Tips' page includes tips for mental health and tips for exercises, we implemented a dropdown there. This way, the viewer can easily choose where they want tips for. On the exercise page, we also created a 'Learn More' button to make it easier for the viewer to immediately read more about the exercises we created, instead of going to another page to find out.
  • The structure of the navigation and content
    • # Pages Visual Justification
    1. Homepage Paragraph and Picture We chose to keep the homepage very clear and simple because our target audience (Dutch football players) is masculine and likes simplicity. We decided to add a picture to make the homepage easier on the eye, instead of having a page with only information, which will likely deter our target audience.
    2. Feed Comment boxes For the feed, again we chose to keep it simple, as explained above. We added our blue color here to create more contrast and to make you feel on a safe page. We added our comment option into boxes to make it as easy as possible on the eye.
    3. Exercises Cards For the exercises page, we chose to use cards because we wanted to combine the explanation of the exercises with pictures. This creates an easy way to understand the exercise instead of reading a big paragraph of text. And we kept the "Learn More" button so people can quickly see which exercise they're interested in, and they can learn more about the exercise they want when they click on it.
    4. Mental health Paragrap's  When you come to the mental health page, you can see that we used more text instead of visuals. We decided to keep it mostly text with minimal pictures to keep the readers attention to the text. The picture is added to emphesize the event colum.    
    We divided the structure of the content different on every page. We chose to make it different, because this will make it more convince to look at different pages on our website. When you click on 2 pages, and they look the same it is understandable that you get bored and leave the website. Therefore, we created every page on a different way, this will keep the readers curious. Also, every page has a different goal, by making them with a different layout the message comes across better and the reader understands that the pages possess different content. When we look at the design rules, we can say that we implemented them well on our website. Points we focused on were easy to navigate, visual appealing to the target audience, a clear message and using not too many words to convey the message.     Testing Report

  • The Testing Goals
    • When testing our website we want to gain a better understanding on how people navigate through our website. We want to see if people are able to find the pages and attributes we ask them. Next to that, we want to gain more insight of what our testing participants think about our design and colour choices. We want to make sure our brand is clearly seen in our website and that people know what we stand for. This is why we also dive into the first initial impression our participants have from our website. Overall, our main goal is to see if our website was clear and usable for our participants and future participants. 
  • The Testing Methods
    • We collectively had 8 people testing our website. This meant that every member in the group interviewed 2 people in total. The participants matched our target audience. We wanted to see if the website would be attractive and usable for people who potentially need it.

      The test setting was done in different ways due to the availability of our participants. Most were done on campus but some at home or in different areas. The testing was voice recorded with each team member explaining the testing and giving the assignment while the participants were asked to describe the testing they were doing.

      The testing consisted of testing the website and being asked follow up questions about the website. The question we asked goes as follows:

      What was your initial impression when you first landed on our website or encountered our brand?

      Did the website/brand convey a clear message

      Did you encounter any difficulties navigating the website or finding specific features?

      What are your thoughts on the overall design and visual appeal of our website/brand?

      Do you have any specific suggestions or features you'd like to see added?

      What would encourage you to recommend our website/brand to others?

      How likely are you to recommend our brand to others? Why?

  • The Testing Results
    • Overall, our participants could navigate through our website and find the attributes we asked them to go to. Many said it was easy and clear where to go. The understanding on where the pages are were clear. Even though it took them a moment to find the toggle to go to Mental health and Exercises, once they did find it they could easily navigate through it. Many also said that the colour palette was fitting for our brand and for football. However, there were some points for improvement and change.

      1. Finish the content and the text; without the content and the text it was hard to understand what we offer.

      2. Add more visuals; use visual aid to understand the brand and message we portray better.

      3. Some colour changes, in the feed page the text is not visible due to colour choice.

      We implemented the feedback we have gotten and changed the attributes that are stated above. The content is finished and all text are written out. We also added more pictures to help with the visual aid. We also changed the colour of the text in the Feed page so that it could be readable for our users. Next to this feedback we also got other suggestions for our website that could be implemented in the future such as a moving background or videos.

    • Marketing

    Context of campaign and promotional activities

    In our marketing campaign we want to engage our audience and educate them about our website that will be launched. In our marketing, we mostly want to capture the attention of our target audience, so we chose not to highlight mental health as much as there would be on the website, as it is still something that is a bit of a stigma within the football community. This way, the mental health topic is approachable for them.

    Our objectives (based on our Communication and Media plan) are as follows:

    • We will post reels weekly, as well as stories and feed posts.
    • We want to reach 700 views and 40 likes on each reel, and get 73 followers.
    • According to our calculations in the Communication and Media plan, which connect to Instagram accounts made for people from our audiences, these are achievable goals to set.
    • Our website needs viewers to function, which makes these goals very relevant, as it widens our audience reached.
    • We want to achieve this within our 3 weeks of active posting.

    Looking at statistics, Dutch teenagers have Instagram as their second most used social media platform. Using Instagram as our main social media platform ensures us that we can reach our target audience. Instagram also gives us a variety of different ways to reach out our target audience. We make use of posting pictures, stories and reels.

    Learning Points

    We’ve learned that it is difficult to reach the audience we target. Though we reached the follower goals, the people that engaged don’t seem to fall under our targeting segments. Sadly, we do not have insights into our followers on Instagram, as we don’t have 100 followers. However, by simply scrolling through the followers it becomes evident that most of our followers are women, instead of the targeted male football players.

    What worked well over the 3 weeks of promotion was generating an audience for our Instagram account. We currently have 81 followers, which is 11% more than the objective we set beforehand (see Communication and Media Plan). Our like and view goals were met only partially. From the 3 reels posted, only one got over 700 views, the other two currently have 633 and 493 views. This post is also the only post out of the six that reaches the goal of 40 likes. We assume that this has something to do with the fact that this was the first reel we posted. It is noticeable that the first two posts have the most likes and engagement, and the latest posts have the least amount of audience engagement, even though we kept a schedule regarding posting and similar hashtags.

    Stories were the least successful of all of the types of posting, as this reached the least amount of non-followers. One of the story posts with a quote, posted on March 29th reached 40 accounts, of which only two were non-followers. However, looking at the latest reel post, Mind the Ball reached 371 accounts, of which 316 were non-followers, which clearly is the vast majority. Therefore, we can conclude that reels are a great tool for getting traction from a bigger audience. Next to the reel and story posts, we also made feed posts, on which the reach is much lower than the reels, but higher than the stories. For example, the latest feed post reached 92 accounts, of which almost 75% are followers.

    We expected our account to have difficulty regarding like count and views, but we did not expect our numbers regarding reach and likes to decrease over the time of posting. In the future, we would try to keep the audience more engaged throughout the promotion period and post more reels.

    Future Planning

    In the past three weeks, we have posted according to our content calendar (see Planning in the Communication and Media plan), which was one feed post, one reel, and two stories per week. If this project would continue, we would still keep to a schedule, but add in another reel to be posted every week. We could post more information about the PR event, share tips and tricks videos featuring experienced soccer players and coaches, and continue posting motivational football content. .

    We would recommend for the campaign to include some sponsorship deals with local football clubs, and to deepen a bond with the audience by posting more interactive content. Though the stories do not get a lot of non-follower attention, we would still keep posting this, to keep more of a personal connection with the viewer.

    As previously mentioned, what we would do differently is mainly to higher the amount of reels in a week. However, besides that, it was hard to track down if our followers and viewers were actually interested in our brand and website. Therefore, we posted some interactive stories near the end of those 3 weeks. As this was not according to a schedule and quite last-minute, this did not receive the attention it could have gotten. For future projects like this, we would incorporate this interactive aspect of a marketing campaign sooner and more smoothly into the planning, as it can give great insights for the success of the marketing and brand.

      Validation of Assumptions

      Before we conducted our initial interviews, we did not expect to hear that football players were feeling so much pressure because of different reasons. It is true that it is good to feel pressure while you are playing so it can keep your adrenaline high and your energy levels high but this is a very delicate thing. If pressure overflows, it can ruin your whole performance. We assumed football players would have a lot of technical problems but instead, they need support in order to perform even better. That is how we decided to create Mind the Ball. At first, we wanted to focus on the tips and tricks we provide but eventually, we realized we can offer much more! We did a thorough research on what methods would help sports players perform better mentally and physically and that is how we chose to provide the services that we offer. Of course, there are a lot of alternatives on what we can offer but after our solution interviews, we decided that people are happy with this so we can release our brand and then decide if we need to change up anything.
